    <!-- TECHNIQUES -->
    <section class="section-white" id="techniques">
        <h2 class="section-title dark-title">3 Core Techniques I Mastered</h2>
        <div class="techniques-grid">
            <div class="technique-card">
                <span class="tech-num">01</span>
                <h3>The Fade</h3>
                <p>Start with guard 3, blend to 2, finish with 1. Use the lever between guards for smooth transitions. The fade is the foundation of every great cut — master this first.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="technique-card">
                <span class="tech-num">02</span>
                <h3>The Taper</h3>
                <p>Gradual length reduction from top to bottom. Keep edges clean. Blend with no harsh lines. Guards 3-2-1 with lever blending for smooth, natural-looking transitions.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="technique-card">
                <span class="tech-num">03</span>
                <h3>The Lineup</h3>
                <p>Sharp, clean edges along the hairline, temples, and neckline. Use your trimmers for precision. A clean lineup transforms even a basic cut into something sharp.</p>
            </div>
        </div>
    </section>

    <!-- HARD WAY -->
    <section class="section-red" id="lessons">
        <h2 class="section-title" style="color:var(--white);">What I Learned The Hard Way</h2>
        <div class="lessons-grid">
            <div class="lesson-item">
                <span class="lesson-icon">⚠️</span>
                <p><strong>Straight hair shows every mistake.</strong> There's nowhere to hide. It's unforgiving — but it teaches you precision faster than anything else.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="lesson-item">
                <span class="lesson-icon">🔄</span>
                <p><strong>Blending takes the most practice.</strong> It's not about knowledge — it's about hand control and repetition. You have to feel it, not just understand it.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="lesson-item">
                <span class="lesson-icon">💈</span>
                <p><strong>Always cut your own hair first</strong> before cutting someone else's. Test your skills on yourself — it builds confidence and catches your mistakes privately.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="lesson-item">
                <span class="lesson-icon">🧼</span>
                <p><strong>Clean your blades after every single cut.</strong> Dirty blades pull hair and skip cuts. Oil them consistently — it's the difference between clean and rough.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="lesson-item">
                <span class="lesson-icon">⭐</span>
                <p><strong>Quality matters more than speed.</strong> Materials are not cheap. Invest in good tools from the start — cheap clippers will cost you more in the long run.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="lesson-item">
                <span class="lesson-icon">🎯</span>
                <p><strong>Your first cut won't be perfect</strong> — and that is completely okay. Every haircut teaches you something. Stay focused, stay calm, keep going.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="lesson-item">
                <span class="lesson-icon">📺</span>
                <p><strong>Watching tutorials ≠ clipper control.</strong> You can watch every video online — but nothing replaces putting clippers on an actual head and feeling the resistance.</p>
            </div>
            <div class="lesson-item">
                <span class="lesson-icon">💇</span>
                <p><strong>Different textures require different techniques.</strong> Straight, curly, and coarse hair are entirely different worlds. What works on one may not work on another.</p>
            </div>
        </div>
    </section>
